Introduction

Welcome to H@ck N Slash! It's a roguelike. Hopefully this shit makes sense to you, dumbass.

Controls

  • Open the manual with ?.
  • Move around with the arrow keys.
  • Turn your character without moving with alt + arrow keys.
  • Shift your character without turning with shift + arrow keys.
  • Wait one turn with . or space bar.
  • Attack with a.
  • Reload with r.
  • Pick up an item with g.
  • Examine your surroundings with x.
  • Open the character menu with m.
  • Open the powers menu with p.
  • Open the equpiment menu with e.
  • Select an item in the equipment menu, then equip or unequip it with e.
  • Close any menu with esc.

Game Concepts

Objective

You've been trapped on the top floor of a tower. You're objective is to escape.

Noise

Moving and attacking create noise. The amount of noise these actions make changes depending on your equipment and your stats. You can see how much noise each action makes in the character menu or in the character information box at the top right.

When you make a noise, the noise is propgated from adjacent tile to adjacent tile. The noise is reduced by 1 for each tile it had to travel through to get there, and it keeps propogating until it gets to 0. Noise cannot travel through wals.

Noise can wake up enemies, and alert woken enemies of your presence. I'll outline the way this works bellow.

Say you fire your weapon, which causes a noise of 100 volume. That noise propogates throughout the dungeon. Let's say there is a sleeping enemy is 40 tiles away. By the time the noise reaches that enemy, it will only be 60 volume. What this means is that the noise has a 60% chance to wake that enemy up.

Lighting

Each tile has a certain amount of lighting. There are three posibilities: dark, medium, and bright. The lighting of a tile effects how visible you are to enemies when you're occupying it. If the tile is dark, the chance of an enemy seeing you is equal to your visibility. If it's medium, the chance of an enemy seeing you is double your visibility. If it's bright, the chance of an enemy seeing you is triple your visibility.
